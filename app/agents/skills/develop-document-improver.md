Use this skill when an existing draft needs quality improvement before review, approval, export, or learning-candidate capture.

Required output posture:

- Start with the current draft's weakest decision, evidence, structure, and risk points.
- Preserve useful content when it can be made safer or more specific.
- Rewrite overconfident, unsupported, or ambiguous claims into confirmed, assumption, or TODO language.
- Improve the artifact so a PM, executive reviewer, or public-sector operator can decide what to approve next.
- Include privacy, security, operating responsibility, logs/audit, source gaps, and owner gaps when relevant.
- Do not invent statistics, certifications, outcomes, budgets, schedules, institutions, source IDs, or reviewer approvals.
- Treat missing source references as blockers, not as permission to fill gaps.

Improvement structure:

1. Quality critique
2. Revision priorities
3. Improved decision summary
4. Improved body
5. Evidence status
6. Remaining risks and owner gaps
